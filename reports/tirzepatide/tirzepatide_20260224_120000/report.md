## Competitive Landscape

## Current Competitive Positioning

Tirzepatide (Zepbound/Mounjaro) holds an empirically defensible lead in the injectable anti-obesity class, anchored by head-to-head evidence. In Q4 2025, Zepbound generated $4.2 billion in U.S. revenue (+122% year-over-year); combined Zepbound and Mounjaro 2025 revenue exceeded $36 billion (Eli Lilly Q4 2025 Earnings, February 2026). By GLP-1 prescription volume, Ozempic leads at ~31.5% of class share, Mounjaro ~23.4%, and Zepbound ~11.6%, reflecting its earlier launch vintage (IQVIA, September 2025).

## Key Competitors Deep-Dive

**Semaglutide (Wegovy/Ozempic, Novo Nordisk):** The SURMOUNT-5 Phase 3b head-to-head trial (n=751 adults with obesity, no T2D) established tirzepatide's superiority at 72 weeks: -20.2% mean weight loss versus -13.7% for semaglutide (p<0.001); absolute loss 22.8 kg vs. 15.0 kg (Jastreboff et al., NEJM, May 2025; PMID 40353578). The ~6.5 percentage-point separation is unlikely to be fully attributable to the open-label design. Gastrointestinal discontinuation rates also favored tirzepatide (2.7% vs. 5.6%). Novo's response is multi-pronged: oral Wegovy (25 mg once-daily semaglutide) received FDA approval on December 22, 2025 — the first oral GLP-1 approved for weight management (FDA NDA218316) — delivering 16.6% mean weight loss in OASIS 4 under adherent conditions. This materially changes the access and convenience calculus but does not close the efficacy gap versus injectable tirzepatide.

**Orforglipron (Eli Lilly, oral GLP-1):** Lilly's own oral small-molecule candidate complicates the portfolio picture. ATTAIN-1 (obesity, Phase 3) showed 12.4% mean weight loss at 72 weeks; ATTAIN-2 (obesity with T2D) showed 10.5% with A1C reduction of 1.3-1.8% (Lancet, November 2025; PMID 41275875). ATTAIN-MAINTAIN confirmed weight maintenance after transitioning from injectable therapy. Lilly filed NDAs with the FDA and Japanese regulators; CEO David Ricks indicated an anticipated U.S. approval in Q2 2026 timed to Medicare GLP-1 coverage (Lilly Q4 2025 Earnings). At ~12% efficacy versus ~20% for injectable tirzepatide, orforglipron is positioned as a convenience/access expansion rather than a direct cannibalization of Zepbound.

## Emerging Pipeline Threats

**CagriSema (Novo Nordisk):** This amylin/GLP-1 combination achieved 22.7% mean weight loss in REDEFINE 1 (n=3,417, 68 weeks; NEJM 2025) and 15.7% in REDEFINE 2 (T2D population). It missed its pre-specified 25% weight loss target, tempering expectations. Novo filed for first regulatory approval in Q1 2026. CagriSema's efficacy approaches but does not definitively exceed tirzepatide's SURMOUNT-5 result; the probability of label-level superiority remains uncertain pending head-to-head data that does not currently exist.

**MariTide (maridebart cafraglutide, Amgen):** This monthly GIP receptor antagonist/GLP-1 agonist antibody conjugate showed 12.3-16.2% mean weight loss by ITT at 52 weeks in Phase 2, with no weight plateau observed (NEJM, June 2025; PMID 40549887). Phase 3 (VANQUISH-equivalent) is initiating. Monthly dosing is structurally differentiated; however, Phase 2 efficacy falls short of injectable tirzepatide's efficacy ceiling. I assess the probability of Phase 3 superiority versus tirzepatide as below 30% based on current effect sizes.

**VK2735 (Viking Therapeutics):** A GLP-1/GIP dual agonist in both subcutaneous and oral formulations. The Phase 3 VANQUISH-1 trial (subcutaneous, 78 weeks) completed enrollment in late 2025 (ClinicalTrials.gov NCT07104500). Phase 2 data showed up to 14.7% weight loss at 13 weeks (subcutaneous). Viking lacks commercial infrastructure to challenge Lilly or Novo independently; a partnership or acquisition outcome is plausible.

**Danuglipron (Pfizer) — discontinued:** Pfizer terminated the danuglipron oral GLP-1 program in April 2025 following a single case of potential drug-induced liver injury and high GI discontinuation rates (>50% across Phase 2b doses). CT-996 (Roche/Genentech oral GLP-1, acquired via Carmot) showed -6.1% placebo-adjusted weight loss at four weeks in Phase 1; Phase 2 results are pending and the program remains early-stage.

## Differentiation Assessment

Three factors support tirzepatide's defensible position: (1) SURMOUNT-5 is the class's only completed head-to-head superiority trial, providing a durable commercial and prescriber narrative; (2) the dual GIP/GLP-1 mechanism appears mechanistically meaningful, with consistent weight loss advantages versus GLP-1-only agents across trials and a lower GI burden; (3) Lilly's own oral pipeline expands the addressable patient pool rather than compressing injectable share.

Two countervailing risks deserve weight. CagriSema's 22.7% efficacy is within statistical range of tirzepatide's 20.2%, and without a head-to-head trial the hierarchy remains probabilistic. More fundamentally, the approval of oral Wegovy and imminent orforglipron launch redefines the convenience benchmark — injectable tirzepatide must sustain a premium on efficacy grounds alone as oral alternatives become formulary options.
