# Limitations & Staleness

## Purpose
Transparently document the limitations of this analysis and flag areas where the data
may be outdated, incomplete, or unreliable. This section builds reader trust by being
honest about what we don't know and where the analysis might be wrong.

## Data Gathering

This section doesn't require independent data gathering — it's a meta-analysis of the
research quality across all other sections. However:

### Cross-check Steps
1. **Review data recency**: For each section's references, check the most recent date.
   Flag any section relying primarily on data older than 6 months.
2. **WebSearch** for very recent events — search for the drug name with date filters
   in the last 30 days to catch anything the other sections might have missed.
3. **Check BioMCP data freshness**: Note if ClinicalTrials.gov entries appear stale
   (e.g., a trial marked "recruiting" that hasn't been updated in 12+ months).

## Analysis Steps
1. **Data coverage audit**:
   - Which sections had abundant, high-quality data?
   - Which sections relied on sparse or indirect evidence?
   - Were there questions the drug instruction file raised that couldn't be answered?
2. **Source quality assessment**:
   - How much of the analysis relies on primary sources (trial data, FDA documents,
     published papers) vs. secondary sources (news, analyst opinions)?
   - Any single-source dependencies (only one article supporting a key claim)?
3. **Temporal staleness check**:
   - When was the most recent data point for each section?
   - Are there known upcoming events that could materially change the analysis?
   - Is the competitive landscape section based on current market data or projections?
4. **Known unknowns**:
   - What questions remain unanswered?
   - What data would improve confidence in the assessment?
   - What assumptions were made and could they be wrong?
5. **BioMCP tool limitations**:
   - BioMCP databases have their own update cadences — trial data may lag by weeks,
     adverse event data by quarters
   - Note any tools that returned errors or incomplete data

## Output Subsections
- **Data recency**: When was the freshest and stalest data used? Any sections at risk
  of being outdated?
- **Coverage gaps**: Topics that should have been analyzed but lacked sufficient data
- **Source quality**: Balance of primary vs. secondary sources, any single-source risks
- **Key assumptions**: Explicit listing of assumptions baked into the analysis
- **Recommended follow-ups**: Specific data points or events to monitor that would
  change the analysis

## Writing Notes
- This section should be concise (200–400 words) — it's a quality disclaimer, not
  a separate report
- Be specific: "The competitive landscape section relies on market share data from
  Q3 2025, which may not reflect recent formulary changes" is better than "some data
  may be outdated"
- Don't undermine the entire analysis — most limitations are normal and expected.
  The goal is transparency, not self-doubt.
- This section should help the reader calibrate their confidence in the rest of the
  report
