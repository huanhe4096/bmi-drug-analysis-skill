- **Brand name**: Mounjaro (T2D), Zepbound (Obesity)

- **Company**: Eli Lilly and Company

## Drug-Specific Emphasis

These notes supplement the generic section instructions. The section writer should
incorporate these alongside the standard section guidance.

### Competitive Landscape
- **Primary focus**: Head-to-head positioning vs. semaglutide (Novo Nordisk).
  SURMOUNT-5 data is the central competitive question.
- Emerging oral GLP-1 threats (orforglipron, danuglipron, CT-996) change the
  convenience calculus — assess how they erode the injectable market.
- Dual GIP/GLP-1 mechanism differentiation — is it clinically meaningful vs.
  GLP-1-only agents?
- CagriSema (amylin + semaglutide combo) as next-gen competitive threat.
- Track MariTide (Amgen) and VK2735 (Viking) as differentiated entrants.

### Development Status
- MASH/NASH expansion via SYNERGY program — monitor liver histology endpoints closely.
- SURPASS-CVOT cardiovascular outcomes timeline is critical for label competitiveness.
- OSA and HFpEF indications represent meaningful lifecycle extension.
- Pediatric obesity development timelines.

### Scalability
- Fill-finish capacity is the primary bottleneck — Lilly has invested billions but
  demand continues to outpace supply.
- Autoinjector device supply chain is a secondary concern.
- International launch cadence (especially China) is gated by manufacturing capacity.

### Clinical Trial Evidence
- SURPASS program (T2D) and SURMOUNT program (obesity) are the two core data packages.
- Weight loss magnitude (up to ~26% in SURMOUNT-4 extension) is a key differentiator.
- GI tolerability profile at higher doses vs. GLP-1-only agents.

### Risks and Uncertainties
- Thyroid C-cell tumors (class-wide preclinical signal) — monitor regulatory posture.
- Insurance coverage barriers and prior auth requirements for obesity indication.
- US political pricing pressure trajectory.
- Patent cliff timeline and biosimilar entry horizon.
